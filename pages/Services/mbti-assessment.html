Personality Assessment – MBTI Test

What It Is

A personality framework built on Carl Jung's theory of psychological types, one of the most widely used tools for self-understanding worldwide.

What It Isn't

Not a box. Your type is a starting point for self-awareness, not a fixed identity or a verdict on your worth, or your potential.

What You Get

One of 16 personality types, with a guided session to help you understand what it means for your life, work, and relationships.

1
Before We Begin

A conversation to set the context

Before the assessment, we have a brief introductory session. We explain how the MBTI works, what the results will and won't tell you, and what to expect. This helps you approach the questionnaire openly — without trying to answer "correctly", which is the only way the results are genuinely useful.

There are no right or wrong answers. The assessment only works when you answer as you naturally are, not as you'd like to be.
2
The Assessment

A structured questionnaire, honest and reflective

You'll complete the MBTI questionnaire at your own pace — a series of carefully designed questions about how you naturally think, make decisions, and engage with the world. There's no timer, no pressure. Most people find it quietly revealing even before the results come back. The questionnaire takes roughly 20–30 minutes. You're encouraged to go with your first instinct rather than overthinking each answer.
3
Your Results

Discovering your personality type

Your results reveal one of 16 personality types: a four-letter combination that reflects how you direct your energy, take in information, make decisions, and approach structure. We walk you through what your type means in plain language, exploring the strengths, blind spots, and tendencies that come with it.

Your type is a description, not a prescription. It tells you how you tend to be — not how you have to be.
4
Deep Dive Session

Making sense of what the results mean for you

A personality type only becomes useful when it's explored in the context of your actual life. In a guided debrief session, we help you connect your type to your real experiences — your relationships, your career, your recurring frustrations, your instinctive strengths. This is where insight becomes something you can actually use.

Many people arrive thinking they know themselves well. This session tends to surface things they hadn't quite put into words before.
5
Moving Forward

Applying self-knowledge to what comes next

Understanding your type is only the beginning. We help you think about what to do with that understanding, whether it's making a career decision, improving how you communicate with someone, or simply giving yourself permission to work in a way that suits you. You leave with clarity and a set of practical insights to carry forward.

Self-awareness without application stays interesting but doesn't change much. We make sure it becomes useful.

Career & Work

Understand the work environments, roles, and styles that bring out your best, and which tend to drain you.

Relationships

See how your type interacts with others, where you connect naturally and where friction tends to show up.

Personal growth

Identify your natural blind spots and strength, and build a more intentional relationship with who you're becoming.

A Gentle Reminder...

The MBTI is a tool for understanding, not a life sentence. Your type reflects your natural tendencies, it doesn't define your limits or predict your future. Many people find that having language for how they naturally are brings a quiet sense of relief: not everything that felt like a flaw was one.

A space where you can feel truly seen and make sense of what you're going through.
